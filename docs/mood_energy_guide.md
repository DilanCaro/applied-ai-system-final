# Mood And Energy Guide

This file maps common natural-language descriptions into the catalog's supported moods and energy range.

- `upbeat`, `feel-good`, and `bright` usually map to the `happy` mood and medium-to-high energy.
- `calm`, `soft`, `gentle`, and `wind down` usually map to lower energy and moods like `chill` or `relaxed`.
- `dramatic` can still be high energy, but when paired with `sad` it means the recommender should preserve emotional weight rather than turning everything cheerful.
- `acoustic`, `organic`, `warm`, and `unplugged` should increase preference for higher `acousticness`.
